Chanse Summers light opera

The Pirates of Penzance

Act I — Scene: A rocky sea-shore on the coast of Cornwall

RUTH

RECITATIVE.Oh, take me with you! I cannot live if I am left behind.

FREDERIC

RECITATIVE.Ruth, I will be quite candid with you. You are very dear to me, as you know, but I must be circumspect. You see, you are considerably older than I. A lad of twenty-one usually looks for a wife of seventeen.

RUTH

RECITATIVE.A wife of seventeen! You will find me a wife of a thousand!

FREDERIC

RECITATIVE.No, but I shall find you a wife of forty-seven, and that is quite enough. Ruth, tell me candidly and without reserve: compared with other women, how are you?

RUTH

RECITATIVE.I will answer you truthfully, master: I have a slight cold, but otherwise I am quite well.

FREDERIC

RECITATIVE.I am sorry for your cold, but I was referring rather to your personal appearance. Compared with other women, are you beautiful?

RUTH

(bashfully)

RECITATIVE.I have been told so, dear master.

FREDERIC

RECITATIVE.Ah, but lately?

RUTH

RECITATIVE.Oh, no; years and years ago.

FREDERIC

RECITATIVE.What do you think of yourself?

RUTH

RECITATIVE.It is a delicate question to answer, but I think I am a fine woman.

FREDERIC

RECITATIVE.That is your candid opinion?

RUTH

RECITATIVE.Yes, I should be deceiving you if I told you otherwise.

FREDERIC

RECITATIVE.Thank you, Ruth. I believe you, for I am sure you would not practice on my inexperience. I wish to do the right thing, and if— I say if— you are really a fine woman, your age shall be no obstacle to our union!

(Shakes hands with her. Chorus of girls heard in the distance, "climbing over rocky mountain," etc.)

RECITATIVE.Hark! Surely I hear voices! Who has ventured to approach our all but inaccessible lair?

RUTH

(aside)

RECITATIVE.Confusion! it is the voices of young girls! If he should see them I am lost.

FREDERIC

(looking off)

RECITATIVE.By all that's marvellous, a bevy of beautiful maidens!

RUTH

(aside)

RECITATIVE.Lost! lost! lost!
